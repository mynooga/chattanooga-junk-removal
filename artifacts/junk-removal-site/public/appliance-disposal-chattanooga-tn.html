Why Appliance Disposal Requires Care

Unlike most household junk, many appliances contain components that require special handling during disposal. Refrigerators, freezers, and window air conditioners contain refrigerants that must be recovered by certified technicians before the unit can be crushed or recycled — this is a federal requirement under EPA regulations.

Other appliances like washing machines, dryers, and stoves don't have the same regulatory requirements, but they're heavy, bulky, and potentially dangerous to move without the right equipment. Dropping a refrigerator on your back or damaging flooring while sliding a range across the kitchen are real risks when you try to handle disposal yourself.

Hiring a junk removal company means someone else deals with the logistics, the heavy lifting, and ensuring the appliance ends up at a proper disposal or recycling facility.
